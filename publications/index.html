Multi-Agent SEA: Step-wise Evidence Acquisition for Multimodal Financial Reasoning Abstract This paper addresses multimodal financial reasoning, where models must combine financial text with visual or numerical evidence. It proposes Multi-Agent SEA, a step-wise evidence acquisition framework that decomposes evidence gathering and reasoning across agents so answers are supported by explicit intermediate evidence rather than a single opaque model response.

Neurosymbolic AI: Fundamental Learning for Context Engineering in Computational Linguistics Abstract This paper proposes fundamental learning, a two-stage context engineering framework for computational linguistics. The model first generates lower-level syntactic and semantic analyses, then reuses those analyses as auxiliary context for higher-level pragmatic tasks, improving performance over baselines that do not surface the intermediate labels at inference time.

Rui Mao, Zihao Huang, Xulang Zhang, Erik Cambria

#3 author 2026 Conference Paper

The Evolution of Philosophy: A Metaphorical Cognition Perspective Abstract This paper presents a large-scale study of philosophical cognition through Conceptual Metaphor Theory. It analyzes a corpus of more than 50 canonical texts and about 300,000 sentences across ten schools, extracting metaphor targets, sources, and mappings to study temporal dynamics, author neighborhoods, and school-level metaphor signatures.

Rui Mao, Dapeng Chen, Zihao Huang, Xulang Zhang, Erik Cambria

From 10-K to Industry Multi-Labels: A Bottom-Up LLM-Driven Product Clustering with Pareto Frontier Analysis Abstract This paper studies multi-label industry classification from firms' 10-K filings. It uses LLMs to extract representative products, clusters products by semantic similarity, evaluates multi-label assignments with GPT-based recall, specificity, and overall-score metrics, and selects clustering configurations through Pareto frontier analysis before validating results against TNIC competitive relationships.

Zihao Huang, Joel Quek, Ke-Wei Huang

Combining LLM-Generated Knowledge Graphs with RAG for Financial Sentiment Extraction Abstract This paper proposes a financial sentiment analysis approach that converts financial text into LLM-generated knowledge graphs and uses them inside a retrieval-augmented generation framework. The graph structure captures entities, relations, and attributes, helping the model retrieve relevant evidence and produce more context-aware, interpretable sentiment assessments on financial text datasets.

Application of Attention-Based Siamese Composite Neural Network in Medical Image Recognition Abstract This paper targets few-shot and fine-grained medical image recognition, where limited data can cause overfitting, low reliability, and weak robustness. It builds an attention-based Siamese composite neural network, using attention as the main network and Siamese learning for classification, and tests the model on COVID-19 lung samples.
